In interpreting the results of research, readers always should take note of the time period in which data was collected, given the typical lag between when studies are conducted and when results are released This is a key message from authors of a study published last week that showed minimal readiness among emergency physicians to initiate buprenorphine treatment in the emergency department (ED) The authors report that these results informed initiatives within surveyed physicians' academic medical center sites, which now have demonstrated rapid progress in implementing buprenorphine in the ED
