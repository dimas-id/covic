Community engagement is important for reaching vulnerable populations in the COVID-19pandemic A risk communication framework was implemented by a community-engagedresearch (CenR) partnership in Southeast Minnesota to address COVID-19 prevention,testing, and socioeconomic impacts Bidirectional communication between CommunicationLeaders and community members within their social networks were used by the partnershipto refine messages, leverage resources, and advise policy makers Over 14 days, messageswere delivered by 24 Communication Leaders in 6 languages across 9 electronic platforms to9,882 individuals within their networks CEnR partnerships may effectively implement crisisand emergency risk communication to vulnerable populations in a pandemic
