In December 2019 an epidemic outbreak of a new virus disease in the Chinese area of Wuhan was reported. The new disease caused primarily symptoms of a respiratory tract infection with cough, shortness of breath, and viral pneumonia. The clinical spectrum showed mostly mild symptoms with some patients developing bilateral pneumonia needing partly intensive care treatment and causing death(1). It became apparent, that this new disease was highly contagious, and – intensive care ‐ patients numbers were rapidly rocketing, overwhelming the capacity of local health care facilities.
