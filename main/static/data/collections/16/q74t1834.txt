The fundamental ethical, legal, and policy challenge of public health is balancing public and individual interests, often conceptualized as the conflict between utilitarianism and libertarianism. During the COVID-19 (coronavirus disease 2019) pandemic, this struggle has involved the imposition of extraordinary levels of government-mandated social distancing to protect public health followed by impassioned efforts to lessen these constraints in the interests of individual liberty and economic renewal. (Am J Public Health. Published online ahead of print July 2, 2020: e1-e2. doi:10.2105/AJPH.2020.305849).
