The COVID-19 pandemic requires a new approach to point-of-care ultrasound (POCUS) education. Although virtual education offers a unique solution to didactic material, education that require hands-on learning is limited. POCUS education is predominantly done at the bedside and as a result could pose risk to health-care providers and patients as well as increase utilization of personal protective equipment (PPE). This paper discusses the challenges and unique solutions to address POCUS education in the era of COVID-19.
