One of the most consistent abnormal haemostatic laboratory markers in COVID‐19 is raised D‐dimers. Increased D‐dimers have also been observed in several studies published in the Journal of Thrombosis and Haemostasis to have prognostic implications.(1,2) But some of the perplexing questions in this regard are what may be the reasons for such marked elevation in D‐dimers and may it have any ‘useful’ purpose apart from prognostication?
