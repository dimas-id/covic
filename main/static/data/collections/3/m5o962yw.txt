According to data from the World Health Organization, Italy has been particularly affected by the ongoing COVID‐19 pandemic. On April 1(st) 2020, Italy gained, at a world level, the highest number of total confirmed cases (n=110,574) and deaths (n=13,155) since the beginning of the outbreak. The number of cases raised exponentially, reaching a total of 227,364 infected subjects and 32,330 deaths on May, 20. The distribution of infected subjects and deaths, however, was not homogeneous, being respectively about 7‐times and 12‐times higher in northern‐ than in southern regions
