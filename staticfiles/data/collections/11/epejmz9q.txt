Primary mediastinal B-cell lymphoma (PMBL) and gray-zone lymphoma (GZL) are rare B-cell lymphomas with features intermediate between diffuse large B-cell lymphoma and classical Hodgkin Lymphoma. The fine-needle cytology of a GZL observed in a cervical lymph node is described and compared to the corresponding histological features. This case highlights the cytological and histological diagnostic difficulties and the need of a timely and accurate diagnosis.
