Despite being frequently detected as a co-pathogen in respiratory infections, the human bocavirus has also been shown to be a major player in viral respiratory mono-infections in all age groups (1-4). However, although known since more than a decade, cultivation of the virus remains a major challenge and was so far limited to some selected laboratories, which made either use of primary air liquid interface cell cultures or not commercially available CuFi-8 cells (5, 6).
